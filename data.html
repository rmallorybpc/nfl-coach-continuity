Post-move production is the binding limit. Most signings are depth or practice-squad moves that never produce a measurable season, so they carry no production signal even though they count as signings.

What was excluded

And why

  • Unresolved players (11%). No confident id match, dropped rather than guessed.
  • Depth and practice-squad moves. No measurable role before or after, so no production to retain.
  • Zero and minimum contracts. 32% of signings have a zero AAV and 71% sit at or near the minimum. The pay question uses market contracts only.
  • 2025 and 2026 signings. Production data ends in 2024, so these have no post-move outcome yet.
The offensive line

Measured on availability, not quality

Box scores carry no production number for offensive linemen, and Approximate Value, the one metric that does, is not available for recent seasons without a paid subscription. So for the line the study uses the signal it can source freely: availability, whether the player held his snaps and starts. Line production quality is not publicly measurable, and this study does not claim to measure it.

Honest bottom line

A small, clean sample and a small, clean effect

On this sample: no pay premium for reunions (−0.8%, p = 0.90), and a small negative on production retention (about −0.06, p = 0.03 pooled) that survives selection, nonlinearity, and placebo checks but fades on subsamples. Availability is roughly flat. The full record of how the data was cleaned and how the result changed is in the repository's audit log.
