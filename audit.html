How this idea was tested before it was built

Audit

The idea was gated before any modeling. Each gate had to clear or the project would stop. Here is what each one found.

Gate one

Is there enough to measure?

The first risk was sample size. Reunions could be rare. If the treatment cell were in the low dozens, the study was dead on arrival. So the treatment was counted first. It came in at 493 cross-team reunions, and it holds up split by role and side of the ball. Sample size is not the constraint.

Gate two

Continuity, or coach foresight?

A coach who signs a player he knows may be acting on a private read that the player will fit, not on continuity. So the study tested whether reunion signings look different. On clean data they do not. Reunion and non-reunion players have identical baseline production and near-identical age, and reunions command no pay premium. That is what lets the production result stand on its own: it is not a selection or reversion artifact.

Gate three

Is the question already worked over?

Reunions are the most discussed idea in NFL free agency, and the league runs a tool that surfaces the links. All of it is prospective. None of it measures whether the reunion pays off. The nearest academic work studies team win percentage, not individual production. The concept is saturated. The measured question is open.

A correction on the record

The result moved as the data was cleaned

The finding changed three times before it settled. An early cut hinted positive. A name-matched pipeline read as a near-null and reported a +14 percent pay premium. Consolidating into one committed pipeline showed the premium was an artifact of the minimum-salary tier, and that player identity was leaking across 163 shared names. Resolving identity by id and correcting four staff entries tightened the estimate, and a small, consistent negative emerged that survives placebo and selection checks. The full chronology, with every correction, is in the repository's audit log.

Standing limitations

What to hold in mind

  • The reunion cells are modest. A null is a failure to find the paid-for effect, not proof of a true zero.
  • The outcome is cleanest for skill offense and noisy for the line and much of defense. Linemen are measured on availability alone.
  • The pay premium shows the treatment is selected. Controls reduce that, they do not remove it.
  • This is observational. A backward-looking measurement, not a signing tool.
Posture

A finding against the grain

The study set out to find whether coach continuity reduces the transfer cost. It does not, on this data, at any level of the staff. Teams pay no premium for reunions, and reunited players retain slightly less production, not more. The effect is small and the sample is modest, so it is stated with care, but it is worth publishing precisely because the market behaves as if the opposite were true.
