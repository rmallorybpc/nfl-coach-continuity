Audit

What was checked, and what was wrong

This study reached its answer by first getting it wrong in three different ways. The record is kept here on purpose.

The correction on the record

A finding that dissolved under scrutiny

An earlier version of this study reported that reunited players retained slightly less production, −0.06 at p = 0.04. An external reviewer flagged three of the example players as misdated. Chasing that down exposed a deeper bug: the reunion definition never required the player to change teams, so it counted same-team re-signings as reunions. Of the flagged reunions, only about a fifth were actual moves; the rest were players re-signing where they already were, and the small negative was their ordinary veteran decline, not a reunion effect.

Rebuilding the treatment as an arrival event, and splitting it into player-move and coach-move, removed the re-signings and added the coach-move channel that the signing-based design had missed entirely. Three candidate findings appeared and each failed a harder test. An approximate-value cut hinted positive but was underpowered. The −0.06 negative was the re-signing artifact. A coach-move positive of +0.02 survived until the coaching relationship was made position-aware, at which point it too went to zero. What is left, and what holds under a placebo, is a null on both types and both outcomes.

Standing limitations

The result is associational with observational controls, not causal. Offensive-line production is not publicly measurable, so the line is carried on availability only. The coach-move sample, 88 reunions, is the smallest cell and the least precise. And a null is a bound, not proof of exactly zero: an effect smaller than this design can detect would not show up.

Posture

Every number on this site is produced by the committed pipeline from the committed panel, and a continuous check re-runs the models on each change. The earlier error is left visible because a study that hides its corrections is harder to trust than one that shows them.
