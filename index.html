NFL free agency, 2017 to 2026

The reunion premium

A free agent who changes teams usually loses some of his production. This study asks whether reuniting with a former coach softens that loss. It covers 524 coach reunions, read through what teams pay and what they get back.

Headline finding

Teams pay no premium to reunite a player with a former coach, and reunited players retain slightly less production, not more.

A player who signs with a team where a coach already knows him should keep more of what he was. That is the belief behind every reunion story in March. It does not hold. On clean, id-matched data, reunion signings command no pay premium, and they retain a little less production than comparable players, not more. The effect is small and near the limit of what this sample can detect, but it is consistent across every level of the staff and survives selection and placebo checks. The market's core belief about reunions points the wrong way.

The picture

No premium, and a small negative return.

What this research tracks

One treatment, three outcomes

  • Reunion. Did the new team's head coach, coordinator, or position coach coach this player before, at a different team. This is the treatment.
  • Production. How much of his prior output the player keeps, measured against players at the same position.
  • Availability. Whether he holds his snap share after the move.
  • Pay. What the reunion costs against a comparable signing. This is the premium.
The question

Does the premium buy production?

Whether the extra money teams spend to reunite a player with a former coach predicts the production the player then delivers. A backward-looking test, not a signing tool.

The approach

Public data, assembled by hand

Signings from an open movement dataset. Production from nflverse, normalized within position and season. Coaching staffs, head coach through position coach, assembled for all 32 teams from 2013 to 2026, because no clean source exists.

The data

524 reunions

Six thousand free-agent signings from 2017 to 2026, id-matched and reduced to those where the player has a prior team and the new staff is known. The Data page states coverage in full.
